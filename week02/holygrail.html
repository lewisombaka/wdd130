What is Holy Grail

A "Holy Grail layout" typically refers to a specific layout design pattern used in web development. It's characterized by a webpage layout with a header at the top, a footer at the bottom, and three columns in the middle, with the center column being the widest and containing the main content. The term "Holy Grail" suggests that it's a highly sought-after or ideal layout due to its flexibility and compatibility with different screen sizes.

Why Holy Grail

This layout allows for a header, footer, and main content section with two sidebars on either side. The main content section is given priority for space, making it ideal for displaying the primary content of the webpage. The layout is flexible and adjusts well to different screen sizes and resolutions.
